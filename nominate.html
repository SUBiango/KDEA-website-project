KDEA2021 Nomination Guidelines and Selection

The Kono District Excellence Awards and recognition programs celebrate and honor individuals and organizations who have singularly demonstrated an ability to make a positive impact in the District in education, healthcare, business, science, and medicine, community service, the arts and many other fields.

Tips for writing your nomination:

  • Give specific examples of the nominee's accomplishments towards meeting the award criteria. We encourage you to complete your nomination form and all submissions must be in English.
  • Nomination must be submitted online no later than the 15th October, 2021.
  • Nomination packages will not be accepted after the nomination deadline.

Nomination must include the following information:

  • Citation - a brief and specific statement; the basis for the award nomination
  • Narrative - no more than 250 words
  • Related components:
    1. Technical Accomplishments
    2. Outstanding technical accomplishments in their respective works or fields
    3. Service to the Community

Deadline

The deadline for all nominations is 15th October 2021

Nomination Criteria

In addition to the criteria listed above, all nominees will be evaluated in terms of consistency, sustainability, duration, variety and amount of their activities and contributions.

Leadership

This Category honors individuals, groups and organizations that have made an outstanding contribution in leadership role to the internal and or external community.

Specific criteria include:

  • Impact - the nominee has had a significant, positive and verifiable impact on the district and the internal or external communities that he/she serves.
  • Community Involvement - the nominee should have shown leadership within the greater community or in their respective place of work, served through working with community partners.
  • Teamwork - the nominee has involved others in activities and provided a motivational influence.
  • Creativity - the nominee has been able to meet challenges within available resources.
  • Dynamism - the nominee has made significant observable contributions to the district and its people
  • Sustained Leadership - the nominee has consistently applied leadership skills over an extended period of time.

Innovation

This category honors nominees who have made important contribution to research and activity through their innovative skills in the land.

Specific criteria include:

  • Dedication - the nominee has shown a consistent and sustained innovation to a clear research solution agenda or program.
  • Leadership - the nominee has demonstrated qualities of tenacity, perseverance, diplomacy and enthusiasm befitting a role model for colleagues and students
  • Impact - the nominee's work had been recognized to have made a positive impact on scholarship and the broader community.
  • Performance and Contribution - the nominee has demonstrated exemplary performance of duties and has contributed to the district overall through active involvement (volunteer work, etc.)

Academic Institutions

This category honors academic Institutions for the impact and personal growth of students.

Specific criteria include:

  • Commitment, the school has shown a consistent commitment to student success and to developing methodologies, materials and processes with regards the school environment, performances in public examinations, school administration, qualified and trained teachers.

Organizational Excellence and Impact in the District:

This award recognizes organizations that have demonstrated a sustained commitment to organization development and organization effectiveness within their own operating practices. Organizations receiving this recognition may be large, small, non-profit and public organizations from around the world that have shown a strong commitment. Their CSR work must have evidence of demonstrated impact and achievement of expected outcomes.

Award Purpose

This award identifies, salutes and honors companies / organizations throughout the business community. While there are many aspects to a business' success, leaders are at the forefront of their organizations and bear responsibility, utilizing their ingenuity, creativity and experience to keep their organizations sustainable.

Award Criteria:

Recipients of this award are recognized for demonstrating and following a systematic approach to change and development within their organizations. However, non-profit, public, and corporate organizations are eligible.

Award Description:

This award is presented when a worthy recipient is identified. This may be an annual occurrence. Previous recipients are eligible for and may be recognized with this award multiple times provided additional service has amassed.
